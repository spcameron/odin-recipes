Chicken Cordon Bleu

Description

A cordon bleu is a dish that traditionally consists of meat wrapped around cheese, then breaded and pan-fried or deep-fried. Many modern versions of the classic French recipe, such as this one, are baked.

Classic chicken cordon bleu is made with the following ingredients: chicken cutlets, cooked ham, Swiss cheese, bread crumbs, and simple spices. It's usually topped with a rich cream sauce spiked with a hint of Dijon mustard.

This recipe was originally posted on Allrecipes.

Ingredients

  • nonstick cooking spray
  • 4 skinless, boneless chicken breast halves
  • 1/4 teaspoon salt
  • 1/8 teaspoon ground black pepper
  • 6 slices Swiss cheese
  • 4 slices cooked ham
  • 1/2 cup seasoned bread crumbs

Directions

  1. Gather all ingredients. Preheat oven to 350 degrees F (175 degrees C). Coat a 7x11-inch baking dish with nonstick cooking spray.
  2. Pound chicken breasts to 1/4 inch thickness.
  3. Sprinkle each piece of chicken on both sides with salt and pepper. Place 1 Swiss cheese slice and 1 ham slice on top of each breast.
  4. Roll up each breast and secure with toothpicks.
  5. Place in the prepared baking dish and sprinkle chicken evenly with bread crumbs.
  6. Bake in the preheated oven until the chicken is no longer pink, 30 to 35 minutes.
  7. Remove from the oven, and place 1/2 Swiss cheese slice on top of each breast.
  8. Return to the oven until cheese has melted, 3 to 5 minutes. Remove toothpicks and serve immediately.
